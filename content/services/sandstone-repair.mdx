Sydney's golden sandstone defines the character of our heritage architecture. From the delicate carvings of Victorian terraces to the robust foundations of early colonial buildings, each stone tells a story of craftsmanship that demands specialist knowledge to preserve.

### Understanding Sydney Sandstone

Our local sandstone varies significantly in composition and durability. Our specialists understand:

- **Yellowblock Sandstone**: Pyrmont and Balmain quarries, fine-grained, golden hue
- **Hawkesbury Sandstone**: Richer color, coarser grain, from Gosford and Hornsby
- **Shell-lime Mortar**: Original bedding material that breathes with the stone
- **Traditional Carving**: Hand-tooled details that define each architectural period

Each sandstone type requires specific repair approaches that match original quarry sources and traditional stoneworking techniques.
